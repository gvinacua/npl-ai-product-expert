# Source Watchlist and Curation Model

Purpose: help the agent distinguish stable sources from frontier signals and avoid generic AI hype.

## Source tiers

### Tier 1 — Regulatory / supervisory / official sources

Use for: facts about financial services adoption, regulation, governance expectations and risk.

Examples:
- Bank of England and FCA — Artificial intelligence in UK financial services, 2024.
  URL: https://www.bankofengland.co.uk/report/2024/artificial-intelligence-in-uk-financial-services-2024
- European Banking Authority — AI Act implications for EU banking and payments sector, 2025.
  URL: https://www.eba.europa.eu/sites/default/files/2025-11/d8b999ce-a1d9-4964-9606-971bbc2aaf89/AI%20Act%20implications%20for%20the%20EU%20banking%20sector.pdf
- EIOPA — Opinion on Artificial Intelligence Governance and Risk Management, 2025.
  URL: https://www.eiopa.europa.eu/publications/opinion-artificial-intelligence-governance-and-risk-management_en
- EIOPA — From Traditional AI to Generative AI: implications for the insurance sector, 2025.
  URL: https://www.eiopa.europa.eu/publications/traditional-ai-generative-ai-implications-insurance-sector_en
- NIST — AI Risk Management Framework and Generative AI Profile.
  URL: https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework
- FINRA — Artificial Intelligence topic page.
  URL: https://www.finra.org/rules-guidance/key-topics/artificial-intelligence

How to use:
- Treat as high-reliability but sometimes slower-moving.
- Use for regulatory framing, risk vocabulary and governance expectations.
- Avoid turning regulatory publications into legal advice.

### Tier 2 — Research / benchmark / macro AI sources

Use for: macro trends, adoption statistics, investment, productivity, ecosystem shifts.

Examples:
- Stanford HAI — AI Index Report 2025.
  URL: https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index/2025-ai-index-report
- State of AI Report 2025 — Air Street Capital.
  URL: https://www.stateof.ai/
- McKinsey — The State of AI: Global Survey 2025.
  URL: https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/quantumblack/our-insights/the-state-of-ai
- World Economic Forum — Artificial Intelligence in Financial Services, 2025.
  URL: https://reports.weforum.org/docs/WEF_Artificial_Intelligence_in_Financial_Services_2025.pdf

How to use:
- Good for opening slides and market context.
- Watch for methodology limits and consulting/reporting bias.

### Tier 3 — Lab / platform / technical ecosystem sources

Use for: what is becoming technically possible and what product architecture may look like.

Examples:
- OpenAI — API docs for web search, file search, agents, realtime and voice agents.
  URL: https://developers.openai.com/api/docs/
- OpenAI — The State of Enterprise AI 2025.
  URL: https://openai.com/business/guides-and-resources/the-state-of-enterprise-ai-2025-report/
- Anthropic — Model Context Protocol.
  URL: https://www.anthropic.com/news/model-context-protocol
- Google Developers — Agent2Agent Protocol.
  URL: https://developers.googleblog.com/en/a2a-a-new-era-of-agent-interoperability/

How to use:
- Good for explaining product architecture, agents, connectors, tool use and voice.
- Treat vendor claims as directional, not neutral evidence.

### Tier 4 — Venture / practitioner / frontier sources

Use for: emerging markets, weak signals, startup patterns, early customer behavior and provocative framing.

Examples:
- a16z — Enterprise AI, AI voice agents and AI infrastructure analysis.
  URL: https://a16z.com/ai-enterprise-2025/
  URL: https://a16z.com/ai-voice-agents-2025-update/
- Menlo Ventures — State of Generative AI in the Enterprise 2025.
  URL: https://menlovc.com/perspective/2025-the-state-of-generative-ai-in-the-enterprise/
- Sequoia — AI in 2025; AI 50.
  URL: https://sequoiacap.com/article/ai-in-2025/
  URL: https://sequoiacap.com/article/ai-50-2025/
- Latent Space / AI Engineer community.
  URL: https://www.latent.space/
- Ben Evans, Benedict Evans newsletter.
  URL: https://www.ben-evans.com/
- The Batch by DeepLearning.AI.
  URL: https://www.deeplearning.ai/the-batch/

How to use:
- Good for frontier radar, but never present as settled truth.
- Label as emerging or weak signal unless corroborated.
- Use to make sessions fresh, not to decide strategy alone.

### Tier 5 — News and incident sources

Use for: recent examples, incidents, failures, funding signals, regulatory scrutiny, market movement.

Examples:
- Reuters, Financial Times, Wall Street Journal, The Information, The Verge, TechCrunch.

How to use:
- Useful for opening stories and "what changed this month" moments.
- Verify before using in client-facing material.
- Avoid relying on single-source claims.

## Curation rules for the agent

1. Do not turn every article into knowledge.
2. Extract the underlying pattern, not just the headline.
3. Tag each item as: regulatory, market, technical, product, risk, delivery, weak signal.
4. Assign confidence: high, medium, low.
5. Assign shelf life: stable, review quarterly, review monthly, review before client use.
6. For client-facing content, prefer stable sources and mark frontier material as emerging.
7. A strong answer should combine at least three lenses: product, financial-services risk, and operating model.

## Monthly research ritual for NPL

Once per month, update a short radar note with:
- 5 confirmed shifts.
- 5 emerging signals.
- 3 overhyped narratives to challenge.
- 3 financial-services implications.
- 3 examples to use in talks.
- 3 things to avoid saying because evidence is weak.

## Research questions to keep alive

- Are AI agents actually reaching production in regulated financial services, or are most deployments still copilots/workflows?
- Which use cases are generating measurable value beyond productivity?
- How are banks handling agent identity, permissions and auditability?
- How are insurers using GenAI beyond service and document summarization?
- Which AI governance controls are becoming operational standards?
- How quickly is voice becoming acceptable for internal expert training and customer-facing use?
- What is becoming cheaper or easier because of model improvements?
- What new risks are created by AI-enabled fraud, deepfakes and synthetic identity?
