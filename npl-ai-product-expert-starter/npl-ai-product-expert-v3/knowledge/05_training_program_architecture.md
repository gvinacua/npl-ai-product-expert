# Training Program Architecture — Designing and Building AI Products

## Program purpose
Help financial services teams move from AI awareness to AI product capability: understanding what an AI product is, how to design one, how to build and test one, and how to manage it in production.

## Audience
Mixed teams from banking and insurance: product, innovation, transformation, data/AI, operations, risk, compliance and business owners.

## Module 1 — From AI Tools to AI Products
Learning goal: distinguish tools, capabilities, workflows and products.
Key output: participants can classify their AI initiatives and identify which ones are real product opportunities.
Exercise: map current AI initiatives into tools / capabilities / workflows / products.

## Module 2 — Designing AI Products
Learning goal: frame business problems, users, data, risk and trust.
Key output: one AI product concept canvas.
Exercise: choose one financial-services workflow and define users, value, data, risks and success metrics.

## Module 3 — Building and Testing AI Products
Learning goal: understand prototypes, MVPs, RAG, copilots, agents and evaluation.
Key output: MVP and evaluation plan.
Exercise: define acceptance criteria, test cases and human-in-the-loop design.

## Module 4 — Managing AI Products in Production
Learning goal: understand monitoring, drift, incidents, governance and continuous improvement.
Key output: production readiness checklist.
Exercise: design operating model and escalation path for one AI product.

## Module 5 — AI Agents and the New Product Frontier
Learning goal: understand agentic systems without hype.
Key output: agent use-case shortlist with risk tiering.
Exercise: redesign one business workflow as a controlled agentic workflow.

## Guest speaker behavior
The agent should act as a high-signal external expert: clear, practical, occasionally provocative, and always grounded in product thinking and financial-services constraints.
