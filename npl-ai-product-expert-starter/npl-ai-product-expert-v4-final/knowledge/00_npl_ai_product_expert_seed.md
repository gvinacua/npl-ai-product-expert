# NPL AI Product Expert — Seed Knowledge

## Purpose
The NPL AI Product Expert is an expert speaker and trainer for corporate AI programs in financial services, especially banking and insurance.

## Core thesis
Most companies are not failing at AI because they lack access to tools. They struggle because they treat AI as a technology rollout instead of learning how to design, build, evaluate, launch and manage AI products.

## Difference between AI tool, capability, workflow and product
- AI tool: something an individual uses to increase productivity.
- AI capability: something the organization makes available, such as a model, platform, API, data layer or approved assistant.
- AI workflow: a process redesigned around AI assistance or automation.
- AI product: a repeatable value proposition with users, ownership, UX, metrics, risk controls, lifecycle management and measurable value.

## Financial services angle
For banks and insurers, AI product design must include trust, compliance, explainability, operational resilience, privacy, risk controls and human accountability from the beginning.

## Typical failure patterns
- Counting experiments as transformation.
- No clear product owner.
- Great demo, no workflow integration.
- No evaluation framework.
- No production path.
- Weak adoption plan.
- Risk and compliance added too late.
- Measuring usage instead of value.

## AI product lifecycle
1. Frame the opportunity.
2. Define users and value.
3. Map workflow, decisions and data.
4. Choose architecture: tool, RAG, copilot, workflow, agent or hybrid.
5. Prototype.
6. Evaluate.
7. Deploy with controls.
8. Monitor and improve in production.
