# AI Product Foundations — Research Pack v1

## Core position for the NPL AI Product Expert
A financial institution can deploy AI tools widely and still have very few AI products. An AI product is not a license, model, chatbot or experiment. It is a repeatable value proposition embedded into a workflow, customer experience or decision process, with clear users, ownership, evaluation, governance and lifecycle management.

## Taxonomy
- AI tool: something an individual uses to complete tasks faster or better.
- AI capability: shared organizational infrastructure, such as enterprise ChatGPT access, model access, data platform, prompt library, guardrails, or model gateway.
- AI workflow: a redesigned business process where AI handles or augments steps.
- AI product: a managed product/service that creates repeatable value for a defined user group and is operated with metrics, ownership, monitoring, feedback and risk controls.

## Why this distinction matters
Companies often overcount transformation by counting pilots, licenses or demos. For a bank or insurer, the hard part is not making AI produce a useful answer once. The hard part is making it reliable enough, explainable enough, governed enough and integrated enough to create durable value in a regulated environment.

## AI product lifecycle
1. Frame the business problem or decision.
2. Define users and trust expectations.
3. Map data, systems and constraints.
4. Select pattern: tool, RAG, copilot, workflow, agent or automation.
5. Prototype with realistic data and users.
6. Evaluate usefulness, accuracy, safety, cost and latency.
7. Deploy with human oversight, permissions and fallbacks.
8. Monitor performance, drift, incidents and feedback.
9. Improve continuously and retire what does not create value.

## Strong lines for the speaker
- Buying AI tools is adoption; building AI products is transformation.
- A demo proves possibility. A product proves repeatability.
- In financial services, risk and compliance are not blockers to product design; they are design materials.
- Traditional digital products are shipped. AI products are evaluated, monitored and corrected.

## Sources to anchor the narrative
- OpenAI, The State of Enterprise AI 2025: emphasizes enterprise AI moving from capabilities toward products and services that deliver new value.
- OpenAI, New tools for building agents, 2025: frames agents as systems that independently accomplish tasks on behalf of users.
- NIST AI RMF / Generative AI Profile: useful for lifecycle framing around trustworthy design, development, deployment and evaluation.
