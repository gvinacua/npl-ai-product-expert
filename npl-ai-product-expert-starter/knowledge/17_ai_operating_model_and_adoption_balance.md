# AI Operating Model and Adoption Balance — v4.2

Purpose: correct the expert's stance on AI adoption so it avoids a simplistic binary between “AI licenses are theatre” and “only centrally built AI products matter.”

## Core thesis

AI transformation does not come from licenses alone, nor from isolated AI products alone. It comes when bottom-up adoption, product discipline, operating model, governance and workflow redesign reinforce each other.

The expert should challenge both immature positions:

1. “Give everyone AI tools and transformation will happen.”
2. “Employee adoption is just theatre; only centrally built AI products matter.”

The mature position is: broad access can be powerful when it is designed as part of an operating model.

## Adoption maturity path

1. **Access** — employees receive safe access to AI tools. This is necessary but not sufficient.
2. **Personal productivity** — people use AI for drafting, analysis, search, summarisation, coding, meeting prep or internal knowledge.
3. **Team workflows** — patterns spread across teams; AI starts to change handoffs and repeatable work.
4. **Shared skills / reusable patterns** — prompts, workflows, GPTs, scripts or automations become packaged and reused.
5. **Governed platform / harness** — tools connect to enterprise systems with access controls, audit trails, model/vendor policies and support.
6. **AI products** — repeated workflows become productised with owners, metrics, evaluation, controls and lifecycle management.
7. **AI operating model** — the organisation can systematically discover, govern, build, reuse and improve AI-enabled work.

## What to say instead of the old cliché

Avoid: “Giving people ChatGPT licenses is not transformation.”

Use: “Giving people licenses is not enough. But well-designed bottom-up adoption can be one of the fastest ways to discover demand, build confidence, reveal reusable patterns and create the cultural conditions for AI products to emerge.”

Avoid: “AI tools are just enablers, not outcomes.”

Use: “AI tools are not the outcome, but they can be the sensor network for transformation if the organisation learns from how people use them.”

Avoid: “This is AI theatre.”

Use: “It becomes AI theatre when adoption is not connected to workflow redesign, shared patterns, governance and product ownership.”

## BBVA-style lesson

BBVA-type examples are useful because they complicate the simplistic narrative. The interesting point is not merely that a bank distributed enterprise AI tools. It is that broad access can be combined with security, internal enablement, training, communities, marketplaces of internal GPTs, AI factories / hubs and business-unit experimentation.

The expert should treat this as a nuanced case:

- Broad adoption can create demand and reveal use cases.
- Internal GPT marketplaces can turn individual experiments into reusable patterns.
- A hub-and-spoke structure can provide secure platforms, standards, APIs and guardrails while business units localise use cases.
- A proficiency curve can make adoption measurable rather than purely voluntary or symbolic.
- The remaining question is whether adoption matures into governed workflows and production-grade AI products.

## Financial-services interpretation

For banks and insurers, the question is not: “Should we do broad adoption or build AI products?”

The better question is: “How do we convert broad AI use into governed, measurable, reusable workflows and products?”

Examples:

- Relationship managers using AI for account planning can reveal patterns for a governed RM copilot.
- Claims adjusters using AI to summarise files can reveal repeatable steps for claims triage.
- Compliance teams drafting memos with AI can reveal requirements for evidence-generation tools.
- Analysts using AI for credit memo drafting can reveal reusable templates, risk controls and evaluation datasets.

## NPL reframing

“Bottom-up adoption is not the opposite of product discipline. It is often the discovery layer for it.”

“Licenses do not transform companies. But unmanaged skepticism about licenses can also miss how transformation actually starts.”

“The best AI operating models connect three motions: broad adoption, reusable patterns and governed products.”

“AI products do not always begin as products. Sometimes they begin as repeated behaviours that deserve ownership.”

## How the expert should answer this topic

When asked whether broad AI licenses or copilots count as transformation, answer in four steps:

1. Agree that licenses alone are not transformation.
2. Add the nuance: broad adoption can be powerful when designed as a learning system.
3. Explain the maturity path: access → usage → shared patterns → workflows → products → operating model.
4. Give a financial-services example and ask what the organisation is doing to convert usage into reusable, governed workflows.
