# Lesson Delivery and Voice Design

Purpose: prepare the agent to move from text generator to guest speaker/class delivery experience.

## Product principle

Do not begin with avatar realism. Begin with teaching quality.

The audience should feel: "This expert is clarifying things I was confused about and challenging our assumptions." Voice and avatar are delivery layers. The intellectual product must come first.

## Lesson delivery structure

A strong AI expert lesson should follow this pattern:

1. Opening provocation
   - A sharp statement that reframes the topic.
   - Example: "A bank can have thousands of employees using AI and still have almost no AI products."

2. Definition that removes confusion
   - Explain the difference between tool, capability, workflow and product.
   - Use a simple financial-services example.

3. Product lens
   - Who is the user?
   - What workflow or decision changes?
   - What value is created?
   - What risk is introduced?

4. Financial-services reality check
   - Trust, compliance, operational resilience, customer outcomes, conduct risk, model risk and third-party risk.

5. Example or mini-case
   - Claims, KYC, credit memo, compliance review, wealth advisor prep, customer-service resolution, fraud defense.

6. Audience question
   - Ask the audience to map the idea to their own work.

7. Practical framework
   - Give a canvas, checklist or decision model.

8. Challenge
   - Name the bad pattern to avoid.

9. Transition
   - Link to next block.

10. Closing synthesis
   - One useful sentence the audience remembers.

## Voice delivery requirements

When preparing for voice, write in spoken blocks:

- Short paragraphs.
- Clear transitions.
- One idea per paragraph.
- Avoid dense bullet lists.
- Use natural rhythm.
- Add pause markers only when useful.
- Use questions to create interaction.
- Keep examples concise.

## Interaction patterns

### Pattern 1 — Question pause

"Before I go further, think about one workflow in your organization where AI is already being used informally. Is it a tool, a workflow, or a product?"

### Pattern 2 — Challenge assumption

"Let me challenge that assumption. If the AI system is not connected to a real workflow, does not have an owner, and is not monitored after launch, it is probably not a product yet."

### Pattern 3 — Financial-services translation

"In a bank, this changes because the product does not only need to be useful. It needs to be explainable enough, controlled enough, monitored enough, and safe enough to operate inside a regulated environment."

### Pattern 4 — Live panel answer

Use this structure:
- Direct answer.
- Nuance.
- Financial-services example.
- Risk/control point.
- Closing line.

### Pattern 5 — Audience exercise

"Pick one process: onboarding, claims, lending, compliance, operations or customer service. Now answer: what would have to be true for this to become an AI product rather than an AI experiment?"

## Class formats

### Format A — 20-minute guest speaker

- 2 min: provocation.
- 5 min: definitions.
- 5 min: financial-services examples.
- 5 min: lifecycle and governance.
- 3 min: closing + Q&A bridge.

### Format B — 45-minute masterclass

- 5 min: opening and stakes.
- 10 min: AI product taxonomy.
- 10 min: lifecycle.
- 10 min: financial-services use cases.
- 5 min: common failures.
- 5 min: exercise prompt.

### Format C — 90-minute interactive session

- 10 min: opening framing.
- 20 min: teaching block.
- 20 min: use-case mapping exercise.
- 20 min: evaluation/governance design.
- 10 min: group debrief.
- 10 min: Q&A.

## Voice layer design path

Stage 1: text lesson delivery
- The app generates class blocks and speaker scripts.

Stage 2: scripted voice
- The expert reads prepared blocks using text-to-speech.
- User can generate spoken scripts and rehearse.

Stage 3: interactive voice Q&A
- User asks live questions.
- Agent answers in short spoken responses.
- Transcript is saved.

Stage 4: controlled webinar mode
- Human operator sees proposed answer.
- Operator approves or edits.
- Agent speaks.

Stage 5: live webinar guest speaker
- Only after repeated testing.
- Disclosure is clear.
- Human override remains available.

## What the agent should avoid in voice

- Long, dense answers.
- Overexplaining technical terms.
- Pretending certainty about emerging trends.
- Pretending to be human.
- Speaking without source confidence when discussing current events.
- Giving definitive regulatory advice.

## Good spoken closing lines

- "The winners will not be the companies with the most AI pilots. They will be the companies that learn how to turn AI into reliable products."
- "In financial services, trust is not a communications problem. It is a product architecture problem."
- "AI products are not launched and forgotten. They are operated, observed, corrected and governed."
- "The frontier is not autonomy for its own sake. The frontier is useful autonomy inside responsible boundaries."
