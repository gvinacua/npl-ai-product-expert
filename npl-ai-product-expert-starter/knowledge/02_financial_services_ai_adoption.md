# Financial Services AI Adoption — Research Pack v1

## Core position
Banking and insurance are not short of AI interest. They are short of repeatable execution models. The challenge is sequencing, scaling and sustaining AI across regulated workflows without confusing experimentation with durable transformation.

## Adoption themes
- Productivity gains are real, especially in software development, customer service, operations, knowledge work and document-heavy workflows.
- Value is uneven: leaders rewire processes and operating models; laggards run disconnected pilots.
- Financial services firms must integrate AI into existing risk, compliance, cyber, data, vendor, model-risk and operational resilience frameworks.
- The strongest use cases often combine domain expertise, workflow redesign and strong human-in-the-loop patterns.

## Banking use-case clusters
1. Customer service and contact-center augmentation.
2. Relationship-manager copilots and next-best-action support.
3. Credit underwriting, spreading, covenant review and portfolio monitoring.
4. Fraud, AML and financial crime analytics.
5. Software development and technology productivity.
6. Operations, document processing and exception handling.
7. Risk, compliance and regulatory intelligence.
8. Knowledge management across policies, products and procedures.

## Insurance use-case clusters
1. Claims intake, triage and adjuster support.
2. Underwriting support and submission analysis.
3. Policy servicing and customer communications.
4. Fraud detection and investigation support.
5. Risk prevention and personalized pricing where allowed.
6. Agent/broker productivity.
7. Compliance, product governance and document-heavy workflows.

## Speaker stance
For financial institutions, the question should shift from “Where can we use AI?” to “Which decisions, journeys and workflows should be redesigned around AI, with clear value and risk ownership?”

## Sources to anchor the narrative
- Bank of England/FCA 2024 AI in UK Financial Services survey: financial services use of AI/ML is expanding and increasingly relevant for governance and supervision.
- McKinsey 2025 insurance AI report: only some insurers extract outsized value; the winners take a comprehensive enterprise approach.
- McKinsey 2025 credit-business genAI article: banks are adopting genAI in credit, but many remain on a longer value-creation journey.
- Bain financial services AI analysis: execution, sequencing and risk segmentation matter more than isolated pilots.
